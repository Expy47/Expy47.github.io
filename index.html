About Me

I am Junaid Ul Islam, a civil engineering graduate from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology and currently working as a Lecturer in the Department of Civil Engineering at Bangladesh University of Business and Technology. My academic and research interests are mainly focused on transportation engineering, traffic flow theory, travel behavior modeling, traffic simulation, and adaptive signal control.

My long-term goal is to contribute to the development of efficient, safe, and intelligent transportation systems, particularly for rapidly growing and highly congested urban areas.

Research Experience

Access-Egress Mode Choice Modeling for Dhaka MRT Line 6

My undergraduate thesis focused on access and egress mode choice behavior for Dhaka’s first mass rapid transit system. The study used revealed and stated preference data and applied discrete choice models to understand passengers’ access and egress travel behavior.

RISE Grant Research Project

I worked on a research project supported by the Research and Innovation Centre for Science and Engineering. The project involved travel behavior data collection, model development, and interpretation of access-egress mode choice patterns.

Traffic Signal Control and Simulation

I am independently studying traffic flow theory, traffic simulation, reinforcement learning, and max-pressure control to explore adaptive traffic signal strategies for congested urban networks.

Projects

Redevelopment of Signboard Pedestrian Overbridge

Led a capstone project on improving pedestrian accessibility and safety at Signboard intersection by proposing an extended pedestrian overbridge design and related traffic management improvements.

Bus Terminal Simulation

Working on a modular discrete-event simulation framework for bus terminal operations using MATLAB SimEvents.

Google Maps API-Based Transportation Data Extraction

Used Google Maps API tools to estimate travel time, distance, and route information for transportation modeling and analysis.
